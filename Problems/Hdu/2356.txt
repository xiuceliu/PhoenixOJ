<h1 style="color:#1A5CC8">Rout 66</h1>
<font><b><span style="font-family:Arial;font-size:12px;font-weight:bold;color:green">Time Limit: 3000/1000 MS (Java/Others)&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;Memory Limit: 32768/32768 K (Java/Others)<br>Total Submission(s): 390&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;Accepted Submission(s): 134<br></span></b></font><br><br><div class="panel_title" align="left">Problem Description</div> <div class="panel_content">While the Romans may have had a fancy number system, the Visigoths (led by Alareiks, known now as Alaric I) managed to take Rome on August<br>24, 410. This was the first time that Rome had been taken by foreign troops in eight hundred years.<br><br>You will simulate a considerably less bloody (and less realistic) rout of soldiers by a potentially overwhelming force.<br><br>Consider a group of invaders of strength I, and an opposing force of strength J, with distance D between them and a defensive stronghold of strength<br>S. The Routing Force F of the invaders can be calculated as their strength times the distance:<br><br>F = ID<br><br>and the Blocking Force B of the defenders can be calculated as their strength times the square of their defenses:<br><br>B = JS<sup>2</sup><br><br>If F is less than or equal to B, the invaders' rout fails, and they are driven off. If F is greater than B, the rout is successful, all defenders of the<br>stronghold are driven off, and the invaders can continue. However, their strength is reduced by removing a fraction equal to the ratio of the<br>defenders' Blocking Force to their Routing Force for that attack. The resulting number should be rounded up to the nearest integer, to ensure that a<br>successful rout leaves at least one soldier:<br><br>I<sub>new</sub> = ?I(1 -  B/F)?<br><br>If the invaders make it through a stronghold, its location is considered the starting point for calculations regarding the next group of defenders, and so<br>on, until either the invading force makes it all the way through the defenders or is completely routed.<br><br>Your goal is to determine whether or not a given invading force can make it through a particular gauntlet of defenders.<br><br>For the purposes of simplification, all defending strongholds in this simulation are considered to be in a straight line from the starting position of the<br>invaders, and must be encountered in order from nearest to furthest away. No strongholds will be in the same location in a given set of data.</div>
<div class="panel_bottom">&#160;</div>
<br><div class="panel_title" align="left">Input</div> <div class="panel_content">Input to this problem will begin with a line containing a single integer N (1 ≤ N ≤ 100) indicating the number of data sets. Each data set consists of<br>the following components:<br><br>A line containing a single integer E (1 ≤ E ≤ 20) indicating the number of defensive strongholds in the data set;<br>A series of E lines, each with three integers D, J, S (1 ≤ D, J ≤ 10000; 1 ≤ S ≤ 50) separated by spaces representing the strongholds. D is the<br>distance of the stronghold from the invaders' starting position; remember that all strongholds are considered to be in a straight line from<br>the invaders' starting position, and must be encountered in order from nearest to furthest away. J and S represent the strength of the<br>defenders and the stronghold, respectively, as in the above equation; and<br>A line containing a single integer I (1 ≤ I ≤ 30000) representing the strength of the invading force.</div>
<div class="panel_bottom">&#160;</div>
<br><div class="panel_title" align="left">Output</div> <div class="panel_content">For each data set, print "ROUT!" if the invaders make it through all of the strongholds, or "RETREAT!" if the invaders cannot make it through all of the<br>strongholds.</div>
<div class="panel_bottom">&#160;</div>
<br><div class="panel_title" align="left">Sample Input</div>
<div class="panel_content"><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">2
1
10 10 5
100
2
75 100 5
10 10 5
50</div></pre></div>
<div class="panel_bottom">&#160;</div>
<br><div class="panel_title" align="left">Sample Output</div>
<div class="panel_content"><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">ROUT!
RETREAT!</div></pre></div>
