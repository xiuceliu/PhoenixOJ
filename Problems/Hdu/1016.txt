<h1 style="color:#1A5CC8">Prime Ring Problem</h1>
<font><b><span style="font-family:Arial;font-size:12px;font-weight:bold;color:green">Time Limit: 4000/2000 MS (Java/Others)&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;Memory Limit: 65536/32768 K (Java/Others)<br>Total Submission(s): 28577&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;Accepted Submission(s): 12704<br></span></b></font><br><br><div class="panel_title" align="left">Problem Description</div> <div class="panel_content">A ring is compose of n circles as shown in diagram. Put natural number 1, 2, ..., n into each circle separately, and the sum of numbers in two adjacent circles should be a prime.<br><br>Note: the number of first circle should always be 1.<br><br><img src="../../data/images/1016-1.gif"><br>
</div>
<div class="panel_bottom">&#160;</div>
<br><div class="panel_title" align="left">Input</div> <div class="panel_content">n (0 &lt; n &lt; 20).<br>
</div>
<div class="panel_bottom">&#160;</div>
<br><div class="panel_title" align="left">Output</div> <div class="panel_content">The output format is shown as sample below. Each row represents a series of circle numbers in the ring beginning from 1 clockwisely and anticlockwisely. The order of numbers must satisfy the above requirements. Print solutions in lexicographical order.<br><br>You are to write a program that completes above process.<br><br>Print a blank line after each case.<br>
</div>
<div class="panel_bottom">&#160;</div>
<br><div class="panel_title" align="left">Sample Input</div>
<div class="panel_content"><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">6
8</div></pre></div>
<div class="panel_bottom">&#160;</div>
<br><div class="panel_title" align="left">Sample Output</div>
<div class="panel_content"><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">Case 1:
1 4 3 2 5 6
1 6 5 2 3 4

Case 2:
1 2 3 8 5 6 7 4
1 2 5 8 3 4 7 6
1 4 7 6 5 8 3 2
1 6 7 4 3 8 5 2</div></pre></div>
<div class="panel_bottom">&#160;</div>
